PRE-OP DIAGNOSIS: Ankle joint pain

POST-OP DIAGNOSIS: Same as above

PROCEDURE:  Ankle intra-articular injection with athrography

ANESTHESIA: None

COMPLICATIONS: None

PHYSICIAN: Glenn E MacNichol, MD

PROCEDURE NOTE: Patient was seen in the clinic. After obtaining written consent for the procedure as well as pre-op vital signs, patient to the procedure room and was placed in a supine position. Anterior ankle skin was prepped with Chloraprep then aseptically draped. Using 25-gauge -inch spinal needle, fluoroscopy guidance and procedure kit, ankle joint was reached. Needle tip placement was confirmed with Omnipaque dye under fluroscope. Ankle joint was injected with mg of Kenalog and total cc 2% Lidocaine an d1 cc .5% Marcaine after negative aspiration. Patient tolerated the procedure well. Patients vital signs were stable before and after the procedure. Ankle arthrography was reviewed before Kenalog injection. No unusual dye spread was noted. Patient back to the post op area in stable condition.
