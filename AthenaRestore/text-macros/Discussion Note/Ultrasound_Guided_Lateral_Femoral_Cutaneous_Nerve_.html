Lateral femoral cutaneous nerve block injection

The patient was positioned supine with the {{left|right}} leg rotated extenally and prepped with {{alcohol|chlorhexidine}} in a sterile fashion.

The ultrasonic anatomy of the upper thigh was examined in a transverse plane using a sterily sheathed {{linear|curved}} Clarius ultrasound probe to visualize the sartorius muscle medially and the tensor fascia lata laterally, at a level just distal to the ASIS.

Images were recorded. The Intended injection site was anesthetized with 1% lidocaine.

A 22 gauge {{5|3.5}} inch needle was advanced under live ultrasound guidance from the lateral, until the tip was just superolateral to the sartorius muscle, the interval containing the lateral femoral cutaneous nerve.

Bupivacaine 0.25 % 2 ml mixed lidocaine 2% with betamethasone 6 mg was injected demonstrating easy spread around the nerve.

Needle was removed and a sterile dressing was applied, and the patient was observed to recover from the procedure.

{{He|SHe}} noted immediate {{partial|complete}} resolution of the presenting pain complaint.
