Biceps tendon injection
The patient was positioned supine and the {{left|right}} shoulder was prepped with {{alcohol|chlorhexidine}} in a sterile fashion. The anatomy of the shoulder was examined from the anterior using a sterily sheathed {{linear|curved}} Clarius ultrasound probe. In a short axis view of the extra-articular biceps tendon and at the rotator interval, fluid collection in the sheath {{was|was not}} seen. Injection site lateral to the target was anesthetized with 1% lidocaine. A 22 gauge {{1.5|3.5}} inch needle was advanced under live ultrasound guidance to the tendon sheath. Lidocaine 1% 1-2 ml was injected to confirm easy spread into the sheath. Bupivacaine 0.25% 2 ml mixed with {{betamethasone 3 mg|triamcinolone 20mg}} was injected, and visualized filling the tendon sheath. Needle was removed and a sterile dressing was applied, and the patient was observed to recover from the procedure.
