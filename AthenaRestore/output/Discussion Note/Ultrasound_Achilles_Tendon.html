Ultrasound Examination of Achilles tendon

The patient was positioned prone and made comfortable. Using a Clarius ultrasound system using a {{15 MHz linear|8 MHz curved}} transducer, the anatomy of the posterior calf was examined. Examination of the symptomatic leg was done in comparison to the asymptomatic opposite leg. A longitudinal scan of the achilles tendon on the {{left|right}} leg was performed and recorded, demonstrating {{absence|presence}} of defect, with clear fibrillar pattern of tendon from the calcaneal insertion to the musculotendinous junction.
Abnormal thickinging {{was not|was}} noted.
Defect {{was not|was}} noted at the calcaneal insertion.
Defect {{was not|was}} noted at the musculotendinous junction.
The tendon was scanned a second time in the transverse plane, distally from the musculotendionus junction to the calcaneus, again identifying the {{absence||presence}} of hypoechoic defect.
{{The identified defect (distraction gap) was then measured in flexion and extension and recorded.}}
The gastrocnemius muscle was subsequently scanned with particular attention to the described area of pain, demonstrating normal muscular fiber pattern.
