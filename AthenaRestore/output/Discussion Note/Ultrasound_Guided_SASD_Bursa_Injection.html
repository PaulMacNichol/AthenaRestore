Subacromial Subdeltoid Bursa Injection

The patient was positioned supine and the {{left|right}} shoulder was prepped with {{alcohol|chlorhexidine}} in a sterile fashion.

The anatomy of the shoulder was examined from the anterior using a sterily sheathed {{linear|curved}} Clarius ultrasound probe.

Humeral head, glenoid articular cartilage and supraspinatus tendon, deltoid muscle, and in between the subdeltoid bursa were all visualized.

Intended injection site was anesthetized with 1% lidocaine.

A 22 gauge {{1.5|3.5}} inch needle was advanced under live ultrasound guidance, deep to the deltoid muscle.

Lidocaine 1% 1-2 ml was injected to confirm easy spread into the bursa.

Bupivacaine 0.25% 4ml mixed with {{betamethasone 6 mg|triamcinolone 40mg}} was injected, and visualized filling the bursa space.

Needle was removed and a sterile dressing was applied, and the patient was observed to recover from the procedure.
