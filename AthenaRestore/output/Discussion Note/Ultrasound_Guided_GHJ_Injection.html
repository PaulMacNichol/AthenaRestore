Gleno-humeral joint injection
The patient was positioned sitting and the {{left|right}} shoulder was prepped with {{alcohol|chlorhexidine}} in a sterile fashion. The anatomy of the shoulder was examined from the psterior using a sterily sheathed {{linear|curved}} Clarius ultrasound probe. Humeral head, glenoid articular cartilage and deltoid muscle, were all visualized. Intended injection site was anesthetized with 1% lidocaine. A 22 gauge {{1.5|3.5}} inch needle was advanced under live ultrasound guidance, deep to the deltoid muscle to the glenohumeral joint space.Bupivacaine 0.25% 1-2 ml was injected to confirm easy spread into the bursa. Bupivacaine 0.25% 4ml mixed with {{betamethasone 6 mg|triamcinolone 40mg}} was injected, and visualized delivered to the joint space. Needle was removed and a sterile dressing was applied, and the patient was observed to recover from the procedure.
