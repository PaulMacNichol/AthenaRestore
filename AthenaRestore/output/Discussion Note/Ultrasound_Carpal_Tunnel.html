Carpal Tunnel Sheath Injection

The patients hand was positioned palm upwards and the {{left|right}} hand was prepped with {{alcohol|chlorhexidine}} in a sterile fashion.

The anatomy of the wrist was examined from the palmar side using a sterily sheathed {{linear|curved}} Clarius ultrasound probe.

In a short axis view the radial artery, trapezium and median nerve under the transverse ligament were visuallized and marked.

Injection site lateral to the target was anesthetized with 1% lidocaine. A 25 gauge {{1.5|3.5}} inch needle was advanced under live ultrasound guidance to the tendon sheath.

Visualizing the needle tip, 1 ml betamethasone was injected, visualizing the spread superficial to the nerve bundle.

Needle was removed and a sterile dressing was applied, and the patient was observed to recover from the procedure.
