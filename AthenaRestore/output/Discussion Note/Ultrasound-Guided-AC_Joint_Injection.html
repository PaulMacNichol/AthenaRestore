AC Joint injection
The patient was positioned supine and the {{left|right}} shoulder was prepped with {{alcohol|chlorhexidine}} in a sterile fashion. The anatomy of the AC joint was examined from the anterior using a sterily sheathed {{linear|curved}} Clarius ultrasound probe. With the probe superior and parallel to the clavicle, the joint was visualized in a transverse view. Injection site lateral to the target was anesthetized with 1% lidocaine. A 22 gauge {{1.5|3.5}} inch needle was advanced under live ultrasound guidance lateral to medial into the joint space. Lidocaine 1% 1-2 ml was injected to confirm easy spread into the sheath. Bupivacaine 0.25% 1 ml mixed with {{betamethasone 3 mg|triamcinolone 20mg}} was injected, and visualized filling the joint space. Needle was removed and a sterile dressing was applied, and the patient was observed to recover from the procedure.
