Greater Trochanteric bursa injection

The patient was positioned lateral decubitus with the {{left|right}} hip/groin was prepped with {{alcohol|chlorhexidine}} in a sterile fashion. The ultrasonic anatomy of the hip was examined in a longitudinal plane using a sterily sheathed {{linear|curved}} Clarius ultrasound probe to visualize the femur and greater trochanter. Intended injection site was anesthetized with 1% lidocaine. A f;uid-filled bursa sack {{was|was not}} visualized. A 22 gauge {{5|3.5}} inch needle was advanced under live ultrasound guidance, deep to the gluteus medius muscle. Lidocaine 1% 1-2 ml was injected to confirm easy spread into the bursa. Bupivacaine 0.25% 4ml mixed with {{betamethasone 6 mg|triamcinolone 40mg}} was injected, and visualized filling the bursa. Needle was removed and a sterile dressing was applied, and the patient was observed to recover from the procedure.
